SPONGE IRON

Sponge Iron: A Vital Component for Superior Steel Production

Sponge iron, also known as direct reduced iron (DRI), is produced through an advanced chemical reduction process that removes oxygen from iron ore. This transformation occurs within a refractory-lined rotary kiln, where natural gas or coal acts as a reducing agent. The result is a highly porous metallic substance, resembling a sponge, which gives it the name "sponge iron."

sponge iron plays a crucial role in electric arc furnaces (EAFs) and basic oxygen furnaces (BOFs) for high-quality steel production. Additionally, it serves as a key raw material in wrought iron and cast iron manufacturing. With an annual production capacity of 1,00,000 metric tons, SISCO ensures a steady supply of premium sponge iron, contributing to the strength and reliability of our SISCON TMT bars and other m.s steel products.
