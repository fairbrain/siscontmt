SPONGE IRON

Sponge Iron: A Vital Component for Superior Steel Production

Sponge iron, also known as Direct Reduced Iron (DRI), is produced through an advanced chemical reduction process that removes oxygen from iron ore. This transformation takes place inside a refractory-lined rotary kiln, where natural gas or coal acts as the reducing agent. The result is a highly porous metallic material, resembling a spongeâ€”hence the name "sponge iron."

Sponge iron plays a vital role in steelmaking, particularly in Electric Arc Furnaces (EAFs) and Basic Oxygen Furnaces (BOFs), for producing high-quality steel. It is also an important raw material in the manufacturing of wrought iron and cast iron.

With an annual production capacity of 1,00,000 metric tons, SISCO ensures a reliable supply of premium sponge iron, strengthening the quality and durability of SISCON TMT bars and other MS steel products.

BILLET

Billet : A Vital Component for Superior Steel Production

At SISCON, we produce high-quality billets that serve as the essential raw material for a wide range of metal applications. Typically manufactured in square or rectangular shapes, our billets are crafted using advanced casting and rolling processes to ensure superior strength, consistency, and durability.

These semi-finished metal pieces form the backbone of products such as rods, bars, tubes, wires, and more. Sold by weight or length, our billets are carefully stacked, bundled, and transported under strict quality protocols to maintain their integrity.

With an annual production capacity of 1,00,000 metric tons, SISCO is committed to delivering billets that set benchmarks in reliability and performance.
